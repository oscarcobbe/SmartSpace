**Generated:** 2026-04-27

## Brand DNA Summary
Smart Space is Dublin's #1 Ring installer — a flat-priced (€139), no-contract, brand-agnostic local trade installer fitting Ring, Eufy, Tapo and Nest doorbells and cameras across Dublin and 11 Leinster counties. The voice is confident, plain-spoken and Hiberno-Irish — direct and transactional ("Fitted in 1 hour. Cleaned up. App tuned. Done.") with no hedge phrases or hard scarcity. Visually the brand pairs brand-500 orange (#f48222) and slate-900 with Plus Jakarta Sans on real install photography — hands fitting devices on Irish red-brick and pebbledash render walls, never American-suburban stock.

## Audit Context

These three concepts directly address the three highest-impact weaknesses surfaced across the audit reports:

1. **Amazon's 33.69% impression-share leak (Auction Insights / GOOGLE-ADS-REPORT.md).** The single largest source of leaked clicks is amazon.ie outbidding Smart Space on shopping-intent queries. Concept 1 monetises this gap by targeting the post-purchase orphan moment — the customer who has just bought a Ring on Amazon and now needs someone to install it.
2. **Phonewatch's 19.91% share + the unclaimed "no-contract" white space (COMPETITOR-INTEL.md, Positioning Gap #3).** None of the seven competitors in the auction lead with "no contract, no monitoring fee" — Phonewatch, HomeSecure and Network Security all gate on a long-term subscription. Concept 2 plants the freedom flag that no-one else can credibly fly.
3. **Active RSA promise/landing-page mismatch (LANDING-PAGE-AUDIT.md, /ring-installation 82/100 vs / homepage 62/100).** The new `/ring-installation` LP is message-matched to the offer, but the brand still under-uses its strongest trust stack at the ad-creative level. Concept 3 pushes the verifiable trust ladder — 5,000+ installs, 5★ Google, SME Winner 2025 — at the moment of decision, where audit shows the LP needs more reinforcement (no GBP yet, weaker trust on canonical service pages).

## Campaign Concepts

### Concept 1: The Post-Amazon Rescue
**Hypothesis:** Amazon owns the box but not the install — and 33.69% of the auction's traffic is shoppers in the exact post-purchase moment ("I bought it, now what?"). A concept that names Amazon explicitly, dignifies the buying decision, and offers a known-price one-week install will intercept clicks Amazon cannot convert into a fitted product.
**Primary Message:** Bought a Ring on Amazon? We install it — €139, this week.
**Tone:** Confident 8/10, plain-spoken 9/10, transactional 9/10, Hiberno-Irish (light) — direct, no hedge, no scarcity. "Got the box? Sorted." energy.
**Visual Direction:** Real photograph of a Ring box (or Eufy / Nest box) sitting on a hall table beside a set of house keys, with a Smart Space-branded installer's hand reaching into frame holding a drill — implied "we're here". Brand-500 orange accent on the installer's polo / van decal in the soft background. Irish suburban hallway context (red-brick visible through a half-open door, or a pebbledash render wall outside). Lower 30% kept clean for copy overlay. **banana domain mode: Editorial.**
**Target Platforms:** Google Search Ads (primary — captures "ring installer dublin" + post-purchase informational queries via dedicated ad group), Microsoft Bing (mirror of Google ad group, same RSA copy imported), Meta Feed + Story (retargeting Amazon cart-page visitors via pixel + lookalike of past install customers). Concept earns its strongest yield on Search where intent is highest, but the box-on-table visual is built for Meta scroll-stopping.
**CTA:** Book My €139 Install
**Addresses:** Audit weakness #1 — the Amazon 33.69% impression-share leak. Converts a shopping-intent leak into installer-intent capture.

### Concept 2: No Contract. Just Doorbell.
**Hypothesis:** Phonewatch (19.91% auction share), HomeSecure and Network Security all require a 12-month monitoring contract or recurring monthly fee — none of them lead with "freedom" because none of them can. The 45-64 male homeowner demographic has been burned before by lock-in contracts (broadband, alarms, gym memberships). A concept that names the cost of *not* signing — €139 once vs €361/year forever — will land harder than any feature claim.
**Primary Message:** No contract. No monitoring fee. Doorbell installed from €139.
**Tone:** Plain-spoken 9/10, slightly defiant 7/10, confident 8/10 — the tone of a tradesperson who's been asked one too many times "what's the catch?" and has finally decided to put it on the side of the van.
**Visual Direction:** Split-frame composition. Left half: a stock-style monitoring contract document (blurred legalese, 12-month highlighted, signature line) tinted cool blue-grey, slightly desaturated. Right half: a tidy, fitted Ring doorbell mounted at correct height on an Irish red-brick wall, shot crisp and warm with brand-500 orange catching the door frame edge. A clean diagonal divider separates the two. The visual makes the message before the copy does. Lower 30% reserved for headline. **banana domain mode: Editorial.**
**Target Platforms:** Google Search Ads (RSA targeting "phonewatch alternative", "no contract doorbell ireland", "ring installer no monitoring"), Microsoft Bing (mirrored), Meta Feed (the split-frame reads instantly in-feed; perfect for the demographic that scrolls Facebook), Meta Story (vertical re-cut with the legalese on top, the fitted doorbell on bottom, swipe-up to LP).
**CTA:** Get a No-Contract Quote
**Addresses:** Audit weakness #2 — the unclaimed "no contract" positioning gap. Phonewatch + HomeSecure together hold ~30% of the auction; this concept reframes their strength as their weakness.

### Concept 3: 5,000 Installs and Counting
**Hypothesis:** At decision moment the 45-64 homeowner is risk-averse — they want to know the installer has done this before, won't damage the render, and is local enough to come back if anything's wrong. A concept that stacks three independently verifiable trust signals (volume, rating, award) into a single line — and pairs it with a real install photo — closes the credibility gap that no competitor can match (Phonewatch leads with 33-year brand, but Smart Space owns Ring-specific volume).
**Primary Message:** 5,000+ Ring installs. 5★ Google. SME Winner 2025. Fitted in 1 hour.
**Tone:** Confident 9/10, plain-spoken 8/10, proud-but-not-boastful 7/10 — receipts on the table, no embellishment.
**Visual Direction:** Hero shot of an installer's hands (in a Smart Space-branded polo, sleeves rolled) finishing a doorbell mount on a pebbledash render wall — the doorbell is fully fitted, the level is just being lowered, cabling is invisibly tidy. Shot at golden hour with brand-500 orange picking up on the polo trim and the doorbell's status LED. Tight crop, shallow depth of field. A small "★ 5.0 · 100+ Google Reviews" overlay badge sits top-right on Meta variants. **banana domain mode: Cinema.**
**Target Platforms:** Google Search Ads (brand-defence ad group on "smart space dublin" + competitor-conquest on "phonewatch reviews", "ring installer reviews dublin"), Microsoft Bing (mirrored), Meta Feed (the install-photo + trust-stack pairing earns the highest brand-recall lift), Meta Story (vertical re-cut, install hands top, trust line bottom). Less suited to retargeting (where price-led Concept 1 wins); strongest as a top-and-mid-funnel awareness/consideration play.
**CTA:** See Why Dublin Trusts Us
**Addresses:** Audit weakness #3 — RSA / LP promise reinforcement at the trust layer. Compensates for the still-pending Google Business Profile (calendar Tue 28 Apr) by carrying the trust signals into the creative itself.
